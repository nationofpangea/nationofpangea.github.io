Overview:


The Order of Pangea is founded around an idea of more personalized, experience-based, and boundary-pushing approach to education of the gifted. The way that our curriculum works is that students will have a concentration, and when we travel to offsite locations, they will have special projects that will contribute the students' knowledge about their concentration and help others. During traveling time, students will work on hands on individual and team projects.


Required Courses:


The only required courses for all students at the Order of Pangea is Pangobian, to ensure that the entire student body is fluent in its national language. Attendance in the Pangobian classes is mandatory until students demonstrate complete written and verbal fluency in the language. However, students will also be encouraged to learn the languages of all of the cultures that the school visits.


Grading:


The Order of Pangea has no grading system; students either fail or pass all classes they take. They will be evaluated based on the work they complete and their contributions. If a student is passionate about the learning the subject and makes improvements throughout the course, they will pass the class. However, if a student puts little effort into a class and is not motivated to try and learn the subject matter, they will fail the class and have a discussion with their PAII and the Principal to discuss their options. The Order of Pangea staff understands that sometimes students are simply not passionate about a certain subject, and they will try to help the student find other areas they can excel in instead. However, if a student is not putting effort into any courses they take at our school, we will be forced to expel them from Pangea.
